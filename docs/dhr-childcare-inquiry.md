# DHR Child-Care Licensing Inquiry — Parents Night Out

Goal: a **written** determination from Alabama DHR on whether a capped, periodic, evening
craft event for school-age kids requires a child-care license. Get it BEFORE the first
drop-off event. Ask by phone first, then send the email so there's a paper trail.

**Who to contact:** Alabama DHR, Child Care Services Division — 334-242-1425 ·
https://dhr.alabama.gov/child-care/ . Ask for the licensing consultant for Madison County
(the county office may be the right first call). Also raise it with the attorney (HOM-98) —
an attorney opinion + a DHR email together is the belt-and-suspenders answer.

**Before sending:** fill the LLC name and confirm the event design matches (≤12 kids,
school-age, ~3 hours, periodic). If any of those change, the answer changes.

---

## Email text

**Subject:** Licensing question — periodic evening craft event for 12 or fewer school-age children — Madison, AL

To whom it may concern,

We operate **[EXACT LLC LEGAL NAME] d/b/a Homegrown Studio**, a for-profit craft studio at
525 Hughes Rd Ste F, Madison, AL 35758. We would like a written determination on whether a
recurring event we are planning requires licensure under the Child Care Act (Ala. Code
Title 38, Chapter 7), before we hold it.

The event, as designed:
- A **periodic** evening craft program (not a daily or standing program) — approximately
  [monthly].
- **12 or fewer** children per event (hard cap).
- Children of **lawful school age** (roughly ages 6–12).
- Approximately **3 hours** in the evening.
- Parents/guardians drop off and are not required to remain on premises.
- Supervised by our employees, who will complete criminal background checks (state/federal
  criminal history, child abuse/neglect registry, and sex offender registry) before working
  the event.
- The activity is craft instruction; we are a commercial studio, not a family home, church,
  or school.

Our questions:
1. Does this event require a child-care license under Chapter 7? We note that a "day care
   center" under §38-7-2(4) is defined as receiving **more than 12** children, and that the
   "day care home" / "group day care home" categories apply only to family homes — so a
   commercial program capped at 12 does not appear to match an enumerated licensable
   category. Do you agree, or do you consider us a "child-care facility" under §38-7-2(7)
   that must be licensed under §38-7-3?
2. If a license is NOT required, are there any license-exempt obligations that still apply
   (e.g., notice to DHR, fire/health inspection, background checks, proof of insurance)?
3. If a license IS required, which license category would apply to a commercial program of
   this size, and what are the requirements?
4. Would requiring a parent/guardian to remain on premises or in the immediate vicinity
   change your answer (per the §38-7-2(4)d transient-care provision)?

We want to set this up correctly before offering it. A written response for our records
would be greatly appreciated. Thank you for your time.

[Name], Member — Homegrown Studio · [phone] · [email]

## After the answer
- File the response in this folder.
- If "no license needed": lock the event design at ≤12 / school-age / periodic (don't drift
  above 12), and complete HOM-114 (background checks, A&M insurance, mandatory-reporter
  training) regardless.
- If "license needed": do NOT run drop-off until resolved — restructure to parent-stays
  (per Q4) or drop the drop-off format. See the "we'll just get one" reality check in HOM-99.
