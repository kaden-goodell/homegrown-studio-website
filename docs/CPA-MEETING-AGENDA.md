# CPA Briefing — Goodell Holdings LLC d/b/a Homegrown Studio

Prepared 2026-07-12, revised same day: this is a BRIEFING, not an audit — the
CPA has only been told "we're starting a business" and handled the S-corp
filing. Most of what's below he's hearing for the first time; the goal of the
meeting is to hand him the full picture and agree who owns what.

Facts: Studio opens **Sept 1, 2026** (Madison, AL). Two-member LLC (Kaden
Scott Goodell + Catherine Page Goodell, 50/50, member-managed). EIN 41-3490804
(CP-575A issued 2026-01-09). **S-corp election filed** (per Kaden). Revenue is
already flowing pre-opening (workshops, party bookings); take-home kit rentals
launch with the studio.

## 1. Entity & election — confirm the paperwork landed

- [ ] The 2553 was filed — **ask for the IRS CP261 acceptance letter** (or
  confirmation it arrived). Elections do bounce on signature/date technicalities;
  worth 30 seconds to know it stuck. Note: the CP-575A lists Form 1065 because
  it predates the election — once accepted, the annual return is **1120-S**
  (due 3/15/2027).
- [ ] S-corp obligations to plan now: members must take **reasonable W-2
  salaries** once actively working the business (when should that start —
  Sept 1?), and losses are limited to stock/loan basis — **entity-level debt
  does NOT create basis in an S-corp**. If buildout is being financed, ask how
  he wants it structured (member loans vs. entity debt) so year-1 losses stay
  deductible.
- [ ] **Operating Agreement name typo**: OA says "Goodell Holding LLC" (no S)
  throughout; IRS and AL SoS say "Goodell Holding**s** LLC." Get it amended
  before a bank/lender/registrar cross-checks.

## 2. Deadlines on the calendar right now

- [ ] **Form 941 due 2026-07-31** (printed on the CP-575A). No payroll has run —
  is a zero-wage return required, or is the account not yet "started"? He
  should answer this instantly.
- [ ] **Alabama Business Privilege Tax** — was an initial return (BPT-IN)
  required within 2½ months of the Jan 2026 formation, under the post-2024
  rules (≤$100 exemption)? Was anything filed? (Test question — he should know
  the current AL rule cold.)
- [ ] Form 940 due 2027-01-31; 1065 **or** 1120-S due 2027-03-15 (per #1).
- [ ] **Quarterly estimated taxes** for both members — probably none needed in
  a loss year, but get that in writing.

## 3. Payroll — must be running by Sept 1 (6–10 workers)

- [ ] Register **AL withholding account + AL DOL unemployment (SUI) account**
  before Square Payroll onboarding. Who does it — him or us?
- [ ] **"Studio Crew" comp structure needs his sign-off**: W-2 at $7.25/hr cash
  **plus** store-credit top-up (~$12–15/hr equivalent) loaded as promotional
  gift cards. The credit is **taxable wages** — how does he want it run through
  payroll (imputed income per period? gross-up?) so W-2s are right? This was
  explicitly parked as "CPA to confirm" in the staffing plan.
- [ ] **Workers' comp at 5+ employees BY HEAD in Alabama** — part-timers and
  LLC members count even if members opt out via Form WC15. We cross 5
  immediately. Policy in place before first shift.
- [ ] If S-corp (see #1): reasonable-comp payroll for Kaden + Catherine —
  amounts, start date.

## 4. Sales / rental / amusement tax — the messy one, needs actual rulings

Current state: Square **POS** catalog has a verified 9% "Madison City Taxes"
tax linked to every item. But **orders created through our website's API
(party bookings, kit deposits) collect $0 tax** — Square does not auto-apply
catalog taxes to API orders. Whether that's a bug or correct depends on
taxability answers only he/ALDOR can give:

- [ ] **Workshop/class fees** — taxable in AL? (Services generally no, but AL
  levies amusement/admission tax on some entertainment. Flagged "unsettled →
  CPA" in our July legal research.)
- [ ] **Party room fee ($300)** — service, admission, or bundled sale?
- [ ] **Take-home kit crafts** — tangible personal property the customer keeps:
  presumably plain sales tax. Collected today: $0 online. At pickup the POS
  *will* add 9% — but our website quotes tax-exclusive totals, so either the
  customer gets a surprise at pickup or staff undercollect. We'll fix the
  website whichever way he rules; we need the ruling.
- [ ] **Kit table packages include RENTAL of tableware** — Alabama has a
  separate **rental/leasing privilege tax** (state + local, own MAT account).
  Does the bundled package price owe rental tax, sales tax, or an allocation?
  (Kaden deferred this in July — it's now live product.)
- [ ] **Refundable deposits** — should NOT be taxed as sales (they're
  liabilities), but the "Rental Deposit" catalog item is currently marked
  taxable for POS. Confirm treatment; forfeited deposits likely become taxable
  income when kept.
- [ ] **Gift cards / crew credit** — no tax at load, tax at redemption:
  confirm, and confirm Square is set up that way.
- [ ] **Local registrations**: ALDOR/MAT covers state + which locals? City of
  Madison and Madison County may need separate sales-tax registrations, plus
  county privilege license (Probate/License Commissioner) and city business
  license. Which of these has he actually filed?

## 5. Books & year-1 accounting

- [ ] Cash vs. accrual election; chart of accounts that treats **customer
  deposits and gift-card/crew-credit balances as liabilities**, not revenue.
- [ ] **Startup & buildout costs**: §195 startup amortization vs. current
  deduction; leasehold improvements at 525 Hughes Rd (capitalize/QIP/bonus?);
  equipment and the **kit tableware fleet** (~120 place settings + hero sets —
  §179/de minimis safe harbor per-item?).
- [ ] Square processing fees, Netlify/software, phone (Quo), mileage for kit
  retrievals — deduction hygiene.
- [ ] Beer/wine license costs (~$834/yr) + dram-shop insurance when they land.

## 6. What to walk out of the meeting with

Since he's being briefed for the first time, judge him on what happens AFTER
this meeting, not before it. Ask for four deliverables, in writing:

1. **Election status confirmed** (CP261 in hand or a plan to chase it).
2. **A 12-month filing calendar** — every federal/state/local deadline that
   applies to this business, owned by him.
3. **Ownership of the registration stack**: AL withholding, SUI, sales tax
   (state + city + county), rental tax account if needed, BPT — who files
   which, by when.
4. **A fixed-fee quote** for payroll setup + sales/rental-tax setup + year-1
   return, so scope is his problem.

The fair "is he the right CPA for this stage" test: a good one will hear
"we rent out tableware and pay staff partly in store credit" and *light up
with questions*. If the response to this whole briefing is "sounds fine,
see you at tax time" — that's when you know.
