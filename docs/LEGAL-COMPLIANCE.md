## Priority 1 — Resolve BEFORE the Sept 1 launch

### 1. ⚖️ Parents Night Out vs. Alabama childcare licensing (HIGHEST RISK)
- Ala. Code §38-7-2(7) defines a licensable "child-care facility" as **any** facility, for
  profit or not, receiving **one or more** unrelated children "apart from the parents."
  §38-7-3 requires DHR licensure for facilities in the definition.
- The "crafts instruction" exemption (§38-7-2(4)f) covers only **civic, charitable, and
  governmental organizations** — a for-profit studio is outside its plain text.
- The transient/drop-in exclusion (§38-7-2(4)d) requires parents to stay **on premises or in
  the immediate vicinity and readily available**. This **covers walk-in sessions and pottery
  painting with parents present** — those are clearly fine. It plainly **fails for PNO** where
  parents leave.
- Headcount mitigation: "day care center" requires **more than 12 children**; the sub-12
  categories only apply to family homes. Capping PNO at ≤12 kids helps but is not a confirmed
  safe harbor (the base definition starts at one child). Note 6–9pm crosses the 7pm
  "nighttime center" boundary (same >12 threshold).
- DHR's public pages contain **no** duration/hours-based exemption for short recreational
  drop-off programs. The pivot is whether a periodic 3-hour event makes the studio
  "established and maintained for the care of children" — genuinely ambiguous.
- **Action: get a written DHR determination or Alabama attorney opinion before running PNO.
  Until then, consider requiring one parent per group to remain on premises (fits the
  transient exclusion), or cap ≤12 and get the opinion.**
- Sources: [§38-7-2](https://law.justia.com/codes/alabama/title-38/chapter-7/section-38-7-2/),
  [PHLC Alabama child-care exemptions](https://www.publichealthlawcenter.org/resources/child-care-definitions-and-exemptions-alabama),
  [DHR licensing overview](https://dhr.alabama.gov/child-care/licensing-overview/)

### 2. Workers' compensation — 5-employee headcount rule (confirmed)
- Mandatory at **5+ employees, counted by head not FTE**: part-timers count, and corporate
  officers / **LLC members count toward the threshold even if they opt out of coverage**
  (Form WC15). Independent contractors, domestic/farm/casual labor excluded.
- With several part-time W-2 staff plus owner-members, the studio is at or near the line.
  **Action: price a policy now; get coverage before hiring #5 (counting members).**
- Sources: [ADOL FAQ](https://adol.alabama.gov/faq/how-many-employees-must-you-have-before-coverage-is-mandatory/),
  [labor.alabama.gov WC insurance](https://labor.alabama.gov/wc/insurance.aspx), Ala. Code §25-5-50

### 3. Business licenses — two separate layers
- **State/county privilege license**: issued by the **county Probate Judge or License
  Commissioner** (not ALDOR directly); required in every county of operation; fiscal year
  Oct 1–Sep 30. Whether the studio's specific activities fall under a license schedule item
  must be checked at the county office.
- **Municipal business license**: entirely separate, from **city hall**; typically calendar
  year. ALDOR does not administer these.
- Source: [ALDOR business privilege license](https://www.revenue.alabama.gov/business-license/business-privilege-tax/)

### 4. ⚖️ Sales tax registration & taxability of fees
- City and county sales taxes stack on the state rate. ALDOR administers 200+ local taxes but
  **not all** — contact the county and city to see if separate local registration is needed.
  File consolidated through ONE SPOT / My Alabama Taxes.
- **Unsettled**: how tax applies to mixed transactions — tangible craft goods (taxable retail)
  vs. instruction/workshop fees vs. bundled per-head party pricing. The claim "services are
  never taxed" was REFUTED in verification — don't rely on it. **Action: CPA or ALDOR ruling.**
- Source: [ALDOR sales tax](https://www.revenue.alabama.gov/sales-use/sales-tax/)

### 5. ⚖️ Waivers — Alabama law reshapes the design (IMPORTANT)
What the sources consistently say (practitioner analyses; not adversarially verified):
- **Adult waivers: enforceable** in Alabama for ordinary negligence IF expressed in "clear
  and unequivocal language," evenhanded, for valid consideration. Never covers wanton/willful
  conduct (Barnes v. Birmingham Int'l Raceway, 551 So.2d 929 (Ala. 1989)). No statute mandates
  specific language; releases are ordinary contracts (Ala. Code §12-21-109).
- **Parental waivers do NOT bind the child**: a parent cannot waive a minor's own injury
  claims in favor of a **for-profit** operator (J.T. ex rel. Thode v. Monster Mountain, LLC,
  754 F. Supp. 2d 1323 (M.D. Ala. 2010)). A minor's own signature is voidable. So a signed
  waiver will NOT bar an injured kid's negligence claim against the studio.
- **What still works and should be in the document**:
  1. Release of the **parent's own** claims (enforceable against the adult signer).
  2. **Parental indemnification** clause (parent reimburses studio if child sues) — Monster
     Mountain expressly left this open in Alabama; attorney should draft it.
  3. Assumption-of-risk recitals (Alabama's pure contributory-negligence rule is
     defense-favorable; documented risk disclosure strengthens that defense).
  4. Medical treatment authorization + emergency contact (essential for PNO regardless).
  5. Photo/media release.
- **Implication: insurance, not the waiver, is the real protection for child injuries.**
- E-signatures are valid (federal E-Sign Act + UETA); e-signed waivers have been upheld.
  Vendor templates are not Alabama-reviewed — have the attorney bless the final text.

### 6. General liability insurance
- Craft/pottery-studio GL benchmarks: **$1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate at roughly
  $280–$420/yr** (ACT Insurance $279/yr; Insuranks avg ~$35/mo). Get quotes now.
- ⚖️ Ask the agent specifically: does the policy cover **drop-off minors' programs** (some
  carriers require a childcare endorsement / abuse-and-molestation coverage)? This was not
  settled by research — do not assume base GL covers PNO.
- Also: property coverage for equipment/inventory, and confirm the landlord's required limits.

## Priority 2 — Required, lower effort

### 7. ADA Title III (applies at ANY size — no small-business exemption)
- The 15-employee threshold is Title I (employment) only. As a public accommodation the studio
  must: remove barriers where "readily achievable" (accessible route/entrance, aisle widths);
  admit service dogs (may ask only: required for a disability? what task is it trained to
  perform?); make reasonable policy modifications; communicate effectively.
- Only the 2010 ADA Standards apply to new barrier removal. Small businesses (≤30 FTE or ≤$1M
  revenue) may claim the federal **Disabled Access Credit** for accessibility spend.
- Website accessibility (WCAG) was not researched — treat as open; low-effort wins: alt text,
  keyboard navigation, contrast in the booking modals.
- Source: [ADA Title III primer](https://www.ada.gov/resources/title-iii-primer/)

### 8. Employment basics
- Alabama has **no state minimum wage** — federal $7.25 applies (matches current staffing plan).
- Post required federal + Alabama labor-law posters (free from DOL/ADOL — never pay a poster
  service).
- If hiring anyone under 18: Alabama child-labor certificates and hour restrictions apply
  (not researched in depth — check ADOL Child Labor division before hiring a minor).

### 9. Local premises items (not researched — verify locally)
- **Fire marshal / occupancy**: get posted occupancy for the party room; matters for both code
  and the >12-kids licensing threshold.
- **Health department**: check county rules before serving snacks/drinks; prepackaged food and
  sealed drinks are usually the low-friction path.
- **Music licensing**: playing music in the studio is a public performance — ASCAP/BMI/SESAC
  licenses or a business-licensed streaming service (e.g., Soundtrack Your Brand, SiriusXM for
  Business). Personal Spotify accounts are not licensed for business use. Commonly-missed and
  actively enforced.
- **Kiln/heat-tool safety** (if a kiln is added): ventilation, clearance, and insurer
  notification.

## Priority 3 — Online/digital

### 10. COPPA — likely NOT triggered, keep it that way
- COPPA applies to child-directed sites or general-audience sites with actual knowledge they
  collect personal info **from** under-13s. A booking site where **parents** enter kids' info
  is not automatically covered.
- Design rule: never collect info directly from kids online; parents fill everything in. If
  that ever changes, verifiable parental consent is required (a parent-completed card payment
  is an FTC-approved consent method).
- Photos of kids at events: get the media release in the waiver; don't post names with faces.
- Source: [FTC COPPA six-step plan](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/childrens-online-privacy-protection-rule-six-step-compliance-plan-your-business)

### 11. Site policies
- Publish: privacy policy, booking terms of service, and a clear **refund/cancellation
  policy** (also strengthens Square dispute defense). Surface the cancellation policy inside
  the booking modals before payment.
- PCI: handled by Square as long as payments stay in Square's SDK (they do).

## Waiver tooling — how to collect & attach to Square accounts

Constraints found:
- **Square Contracts**: native e-sign, signed PDF stored and trackable, can auto-send on
  **appointment** bookings (requires Appointments Plus/Premium tier) — but Square explicitly
  states automated forms are **incompatible with Classes**, which is how workshops (and likely
  PNO) are booked. ([Square Contracts help](https://squareup.com/help/us/en/article/7104-contracts))
- **Waiver Electronic** (Square App Marketplace): templates, online/kiosk/QR collection,
  Square payments alongside waivers, indefinite searchable retention — but does not claim
  auto-attachment to Square customer profiles.
- **WaiverForever**: official Square integration is **payments-focused** (pay while signing,
  kiosk app); no customer-profile sync documented.
- No off-the-shelf option truly attaches waivers to the Square Customer record.

Recommended architecture (fits the API-first setup):
1. Waiver step inside our own booking flows (party modal Details step; workshop booking
   modal) — checkbox + typed-name signature over the attorney-approved text; store signed
   record (PDF/JSON + timestamp + IP) ourselves and write a reference onto the Square
   Customer (note field — custom-attribute definitions are at the 10-cap).
2. Front-desk QR → same waiver endpoint for walk-ins; creates/updates the Square customer.
3. Check `waiver_signed` at check-in; annual re-sign.

## 12. Beer & wine license (added 2026-07-09; tickets HOM-109…112)

Researched for on-premises beer + table wine at 525 Hughes Rd Ste F, Madison. Primary
sources: alabcboard.gov, madisonal.gov, Ala. Code Title 28, Ala. Admin. Code, TTB.

- **License types**: no combined retail beer+wine license exists — need ABC **040 Retail Beer
  (On/Off Premises)** $150 + **060 Retail Table Wine (On/Off Premises)** $150, + $50 filing
  each. Avoid the liquor classes (010 Lounge / 020 Restaurant) — spirits-only baggage.
- **Madison explicitly lists "art studios"** as an eligible on-premises beer/wine
  establishment type. Eligible zoning: B-1, B-2, B-3, MC, MU, TND, UC.
- **Minors stay allowed on premises** — the exclusion only applies to lounge licenses.
  **Servers must be 21+.** No food-service requirement. Hours 10am–2am incl. Sundays.
- **Process order**: notarized city application ($100 + $184 publication) → newspaper notice
  → City Council public hearing (≥14 days after filing, ≥10 after publication; Alcohol
  License Review Committee vets) → ABC background check + premises inspection → city alcohol
  business licenses ($75 beer + $75 wine). **~6–10 weeks end-to-end** — the council calendar
  is the binding constraint for Sept 1.
- **Dram shop insurance is a licensure precondition**: ≥$100k/occurrence liquor liability
  (Ala. Admin. Code r. 20-X-5-.14) before ABC issues. GL excludes it — separate
  endorsement/policy. Post-2023 SB104 ("knowingly serving" standard) rates are lower.
- **TTB Form 5630.5d** federal dealer registration — free, before first sale.
- **Renewal every Aug 1** (license year Oct 1–Sep 30); ~$834 first-year licensing total.
- ⚠️ Unverified: Madison's church/school buffer distances (Municode blocked) — ask at
  256-772-5600 BEFORE filing; could disqualify a location. Also confirm which county the
  address is in (Madison city straddles Madison/Limestone counties).
